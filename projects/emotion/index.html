Challenge An affective computing startup wanted to demonstrate how its emotional biometrics solution could be applied to AR/VR – in 5 days.
Solution I created a series of GearVR apps that made users excited about the possibility of emotion-infused technology.
Client NDA Role Interaction Designer Year 2017 Duration 5-day sprint

How does it feel when an app responds to your emotions?

A pioneer in the affective computing space had built a biometrics product that could react to a user’s feelings. The company’s CEO approached me to help them build an app in 4 days to help demonstrate the promise of an emotionally-aware future for our digital lives.

Framing the Vision

Hitting the ground running, I worked with the team to think backwards towards what they most hoped to demonstrate in their VR demo. Showing the technical capacity of their product was important; however, the experience their product could provide was centered on emotion, so the demo's primary purpose was to elicit spontaneous emotion.

We stripped away distracting details, letting emotion emerge as the lifeblood of the experience.

I quickly realized that the client’s beta prototypes faced a difficult problem: the more detail they added to make the prototype feel ‘realistic,’ the further they distanced the user from feeling real emotion (and the closer they creeped towards the uncanny valley). However, after conducting comparative research, I realized that cartoons are excellent at communicating the essence of emotion while avoiding this problem. Ultimately, I guided the team towards a prototype strategy that was less focused on realism while retaining essential features that could communicate the range of emotion.

No one had used an interface like this before. How can we scaffold the user’s first experience with emotion-based interaction?

Staging & Sound

I used staging and sound to better communicate the essence of the experience. The arrangement of the characters and the initial suspenseful created a comical sense of anticipation as the user first signed in. This was meant to elicit a smile, to which the characters would then react with giggles and joy. I contracted a sound designer to help further tailor the mood and character reactions. The user was eased into an understanding that their own emotions would cause the characters and environment to react in real-time.

I directed a 3-person team from concept to functional demo in 5 days.

As project lead, I served as interaction designer, creative director, and Unity developer. I contracted a 3D artist and sound designer to support production. I was responsible for translating the demo’s business requirements, passed on by the CEO, into creative direction for the demo team, ensuring that aesthetic and art style conveyed a fun yet trustworthy tone. I also worked closely with the project’s machine learning engineer to translate sensor feeds into functional application inputs.

After the sprint, I built additional VR experiences to demonstrate technical aspects of the client’s product.

Much of this task involved communicating certain functional metrics to engineers, but in a VR environment.
